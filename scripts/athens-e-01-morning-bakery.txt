That warm buttery smell drifting into the street is Athens waking up. Bakeries here are narrow, practical, oven-deep spaces built for consistency, not spectacle. During the Nazi occupation, baker Aristidis Kaldis hid flour and baked through the night, distributing bread illegally to starving families. If caught, he faced execution. If he stopped, children starved. This site exists because food is where values become physical. The cultural consequence is permanent: food in Athens means care before luxury. If you remember one thing here, remember this: cities survive only if they feed their people first.
