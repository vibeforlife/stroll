From this distance, the Acropolis becomes legible as intention rather than ruin. Built under Pericles after Persian destruction, it was a political manifesto in marble. Funds meant for collective defense were redirected to rebuild Athens as the visual center of civilization. Allies protested. Athens proceeded anyway. That arrogance ignited the Peloponnesian War. Every column was optically adjusted to persuade the eye. Beauty became political language. This site exists because Athens chose defiant rebuilding over modest recovery. The cultural consequence is irreversible: architecture became a tool of power worldwide. If you remember one thing here, remember this: the Acropolis was built to convince humans, not gods.
