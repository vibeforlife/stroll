This rough stone platform is where democracy spoke aloud. Thousands gathered here to decide war, exile, and law. During the Mytilene revolt, Athens voted in fury to execute an entire city, then reversed itself the next day. A second ship raced the first to prevent massacre. Lives depended on rowing speed. This place exists because Athens trusted ordinary citizens with extraordinary power. The consequence was permanent: democracy revealed both its brilliance and its danger. If you remember one thing here, remember this: democracy is powerful not because it is safe, but because it accepts responsibility for its own mistakes.
